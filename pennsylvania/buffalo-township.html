HVAC FAQ — Buffalo, Pennsylvania

How do I find a licensed HVAC contractor in Buffalo, PA?
In Pennsylvania, HVAC contractors must hold EPA Section 608 certification for refrigerant handling and carry general liability and workers' compensation insurance. Larger jobs may require a PA contractor registration. When hiring in Buffalo: verify their insurance certificate; check if they pull permits (required for new equipment in PA); ask about manufacturer certifications (Carrier Factory Authorized, Lennox Premier, etc.); check Google reviews. Our network includes only pre-screened, licensed and insured Buffalo HVAC contractors.
How long does AC installation take in Buffalo?
A standard central AC or heat pump installation in Buffalo — replacing an existing system — is typically completed in one day. New installations requiring ductwork additions may take 2-3 days. Your licensed Buffalo HVAC contractor will pull the required permits before work begins (same-day permits are common in most PA municipalities). After installation, the system is pressure-tested, charged with refrigerant, and tested for proper operation before the crew leaves.
What size AC system do I need for my Buffalo home?
Proper AC sizing is critical — oversized systems short-cycle and cause humidity problems; undersized systems can't keep up on hot PA days. The correct method is a Manual J load calculation performed by your licensed Buffalo HVAC contractor, which accounts for your home's square footage, insulation, window area, ceiling height, and local climate data. As a rough guide: 1 ton per 600-1,000 sq ft, but don't let a contractor quote based on square footage alone without a proper load calculation.
How much does a new AC system cost in Buffalo, PA?
A new central air conditioning system in Buffalo typically costs between $4091 and $10228, including the outdoor condenser unit, air handler or coil, refrigerant line set, thermostat, and labor. Price varies based on home size, efficiency rating (SEER2), and whether ductwork modifications are needed. Heat pump systems range from $4675 to $12177. Get a free written estimate from a licensed Buffalo HVAC contractor before committing.
When should I replace vs. repair my AC in Buffalo, PA?
General rule: if your AC is over 12 years old and repair cost exceeds $1,000 (e.g., compressor failure), replacement is usually the better investment at $4091-$10228. If the system is under 10 years old and the repair is under $500-600, repair makes sense. The '5,000 rule': multiply the system's age by the repair cost — if over $5,000, replace. Also consider: R-22 refrigerant (being phased out, very expensive to recharge), rising energy bills, and uneven cooling. A licensed Buffalo HVAC tech can advise after diagnosis.
How long does an HVAC system last in Pennsylvania?
In Buffalo's climate — hot, humid summers and cold winters — a well-maintained central AC system typically lasts 15-20 years. Gas furnaces last 20-25 years. Heat pumps last 15-20 years. Regular annual maintenance (tune-ups at $97-$370) significantly extends equipment life and efficiency. Signs it's time to replace: system is 15+ years old, frequent repairs, R-22 refrigerant (no longer produced), or rising energy bills despite maintenance.
